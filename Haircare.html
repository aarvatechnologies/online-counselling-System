Hair Care Routine Guide

Morning Hair Care Routine

Preparation

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair and prevent breakage. If heading out, apply a lightweight leave-in conditioner or serum to protect against pollution.

Cleansing

Hair wash frequency depends on hair type:

  • Dry Hair: Twice a week
  • Oily Hair: Every other day

Too much washing strips natural oils, while too little can make hair look dull.

Conditioning

Apply conditioner from mid-lengths to ends (avoid roots). Rinse with cold water to seal the cuticles for smooth, shiny hair.

Styling

Minimize heat styling to prevent damage. Always use a heat protectant before styling tools.

Night Hair Care Routine

  • Gently brush hair to remove tangles and distribute natural oils.
  • Apply oil or a lightweight serum to the ends for hydration.
  • For long or curly hair, loosely braid before sleeping to prevent tangles.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair to prevent damage.

Weekly Hair Care Routine

Oiling

Massage your scalp with coconut, almond, or sesame oil for improved blood circulation and stronger roots.

Hair Masks

Use a hydrating mask for dry hair or a protein-rich mask for damaged hair. Follow the product instructions for the best results.

Exfoliation

Use a natural scalp scrub once a week to remove buildup, prevent dandruff, and promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care Routine Based on Hair Type

Oily Hair

Use a clarifying shampoo to remove excess oil. Avoid thick conditioners; opt for lightweight formulas.

Dry Hair

Hydration is key! Use moisturizing shampoo, conditioner, serum, and oils to retain moisture and shine.

Curly/Wavy Hair

Use the LOC method (Leave-in, Oil, Cream) to maintain hydration and frizz-free curls.

Chemically Treated Hair

Use sulfate-free products to maintain color and prevent damage. Choose products specifically for treated hair.

Hair Care: Seasonal Considerations

Summer

Protect hair from heat and UV rays using leave-in conditioners with SPF.

Monsoon

Humidity can cause dandruff and frizz. Use anti-humidity sprays and clarifying shampoos.

Winter

Cold air strips moisture from hair. Deep conditioning is essential to keep hair hydrated.
