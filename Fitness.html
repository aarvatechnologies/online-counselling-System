What It Means to Be Fit

According to the Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans, there are five key components of physical fitness:

  • Cardiorespiratory Fitness: Your body's ability to use oxygen efficiently.
  • Musculoskeletal Fitness: Strength, endurance, and power.
  • Flexibility: The range of motion of your joints.
  • Balance: Your ability to stay steady and prevent falls.

Types of Fitness

1. Aerobic (Cardiovascular) Exercise

Cardio exercises improve heart health and endurance. Examples include running, cycling, and swimming.

2. Strength Training

Builds muscle and bone strength, reducing risks of falls and fractures in older adults.

3. General Fitness

Staying active helps prevent chronic diseases and maintains overall well-being.
